Berg Balance Test

About

  • Clinical test assessing balance and fall risk with 14 tasks

Scoring

  • 5-point scale ranging from 0 - 4

Results

  • 41-56 = low fall risk
  • 21-40 = medium fall risk
  •   0-20 = high fall risk

    Sitting Balance

  1. SITTING TO STANDING

    2. Instructons: Stand up. Try not to use your hand for support

  2. SITTING WITH BACK UNSUPPORTED BUT FEET SUPPORTED ON FLOOR OR ON A STOOL

    3. Instructions: Sit with arms folded for 2 minutes

    Standing Balance

  3. STANDING UNSUPPORTED

    4. Instructions: Please stand for two minutes without holding on

  4. STANDING UNSUPPORTED WITH FEET TOGETHER

    5. Instructions: Place your feet together and stand without holding on

  5. STANDING UNSUPPORTED WITH EYES CLOSED

    6. Instructions: Close your eyes and stand still for 10 seconds.

  6. STANDING TO SITTING

    7. Instructions: Sit down

  7. TRANSFERS

    8. Instructions: Arrange chair(s) for pivot transfer. Ask subject to transfer one way toward a seat with armrests and one way toward a seat without armrests. You may use two chairs (one with and one without armrests) or a bed and a chair

  8. REACHING FORWARD WITH OUTSTRETCHED ARM WHILE STANDING

    9. Instructions: Lift arm to 90 degrees. Stretch out your fingers and reach forward as far as you can. (Examiner places a ruler at the end of fingertips when arm is at 90 degrees. Fingers should not touch the ruler while reaching forward. The recorded measure is the distance forward that the fingers reach while the subject is in the most forward lean position. When possible, ask subject to use both arms when reaching to avoid rotation of the trunk.)

  9. PICK UP OBJECT FROM THE FLOOR FROM A STANDING POSITION

    10. Instructions: Pick up the shoe/slipper, which is place in front of your feet

  10. TURNING TO LOOK BEHIND OVER LEFT AND RIGHT SHOULDERS WHILE STANDING

    11. Instructions: Turn to look directly behind you over toward the left shoulder. Repeat to the right. Examiner may pick an object to look at directly behind the subject to encourage a better twist turn.

  11. TURN 360 DEGREES

    12. Instructions: Turn completely around in a full circle. Pause. Then turn a full circle in the other direction

  12. PLACE ALTERNATE FOOT ON STEP OR STOOL WHILE STANDING UNSUPPORTED

    13. Instructions: Place each foot alternately on the step/stool. Continue until each foot has touch the step/stool four times

  13. STANDING UNSUPPORTED ONE FOOT IN FRONT

    14. Instructions: Place one foot directly in front of the other. If you feel that you cannot place your foot directly in front, try to step far enough ahead that the heel of your forward foot is ahead of the toes of the other foot. (To score 3 points, the length of the step should exceed the length of the other foot and the width of the stance should approximate the subject’s normal stride width.)

  14. STANDING ON ONE LEG

    15. Instructions: Stand on one leg as long as you can without holding on
